# Fermat&rsquo;s Little Theorem

Suppose $p$ is a **prime** and $k \in \{1,2,...,p-1\}$.

$$k ^ {p-1} \equiv 1$$


<a id="org1fc5bab"></a>

## **Proof**

$\{1,2,3 ,..., p-1\}$ are relatively prime to $p$.

$$k^{\phi(p)} \equiv 1 (mod \ p)$$

$$k^{p-1} \equiv 1 (mod \ p)$$

$$k \cdot k^{p-2} = k^{p-1} \equiv 1$$
